Heritage is an expression of meaning-making, a synthesis of things and narratives. It is a process of identifying and managing the past and present through continuous negotiation, forming a framework for meaning- making. Thus, heritage is not only material but also a response to a com- prehensive socio-cultural narrative1,2. Throughout human civilization, cul- tural heritage (CH)3,4 artifacts have continuously evolved and iterated, telling the story of human intelligence and progress5,6. Pottery artifacts, such as eave tiles, play an important role in China’s ancient culture and have become prevalent topics of discussion at conferences, such as those held by the Society for American Archeology (SAA) and other gatherings of archaeologists and anthropologists, while continuing to attract signiﬁcant global attention7. The excavation and study of these artifacts are supported by extensive heritage datasets, reﬂecting ongoing efforts throughout history8,9. Visual recognition research on Chinese artifacts has revealed that eave tiles have been utilized for over 2000 years as decorative features in ancient waterproof wooden roofs10,11.Examining the temporal evolution and developmental kinship of these motifs is crucial for understanding the culture, art, and social structures of different historical periods12,13.
