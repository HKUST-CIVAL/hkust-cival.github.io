Effects of View Layout on Situated Analytics for Multiple-View Representations in Immersive Visualization

IEEE Transactions on Visualization and Computer Graphics (Proc. IEEE VIS 2022)
Zhen Wen1,  Wei Zeng2,  Luoxuan Weng1,  Yihan Liu1,  Mingliang Xu3,  Wei Chen1
1Zhejiang University    2HKUST(GZ)    3Zhengzhou University
Abstract

Situated visualization overlays data directly onto the physical environment, enabling context-aware data interpretation. In immersive environments, multiple-view (MV) representations are commonly used to present diverse data attributes, yet the effects of view layout on situated analytics remain underexplored. This paper investigates how different view layouts influence user performance and experience in immersive situated visualization. We conduct a controlled experiment comparing various MV layout strategies in augmented reality, measuring task accuracy, completion time, and cognitive load. Our findings reveal that spatial proximity between views and their referenced physical objects significantly impacts analytical efficiency, while view density affects user comfort. We derive design guidelines for arranging multiple views in immersive situated visualizations.
