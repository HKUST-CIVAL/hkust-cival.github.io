Despite these advances, a uniﬁed framework that jointly and distinctly controls both spatial structure and semantic information for high-ﬁdelity dataset synthesis remains absent. To address this gap, we introduce a new framework designed to disentangle and independently control spatial structure and semantic information during high-ﬁdelity architectural heritage dataset synthesis (Fig. 2). The framework integrates two modules within a Flux Redux40 diffusion model: (1) A Structure-aware Augmentation module based on ControlNet27, to enforce strict spatial-structural ﬁdelity, and (2) a Semantic-aware Augmentation module based on LoRA38, to regulate ﬁne-grained attributes such 2 https://doi.org/10.1038/s40494-026-02446-2 Article Fig. 3 | Taxonomy of architectural heritage tasks categorized by MLLM capabilities. Central to our approach is a cross-combination strategy that pairs spatial-structural conﬁgurations with semantic variations for dataset augmentation. This enables controllable and diverse synthesis while maintaining a clear separation between spatial and semantic inﬂuences. We apply this framework to our self-curated façade VQA dataset. Leveraging 208 seed images, our augmentation pipeline expands the dataset to 1672 images (208 real + 1464 synthetic) with 59,884 VQA pairs. Datasetlevel evaluation conﬁrms the high ﬁdelity of the synthesized images, and task-level results show that MLLMs ﬁne-tuned on the augmented dataset achieve improved architectural reasoning without overﬁtting or negative interference.
